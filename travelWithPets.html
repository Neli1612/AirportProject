Travel with pets

Want to travel with your pet?

Rules regarding the transport of animals vary from country to country and airline to airline. We recommend that you contact the authorities in the country you are flying to, as well as seek advice from your airline or travel agency before planning your trip.

    Requirements:
  • The maximum number of pets that can accompany the owner or an authorized person and that are traveling for non-commercial purposes is no more than five
  • The maximum number of pets may exceed five if the purpose is to participate in competitions, exhibitions or sporting events or to train for such events and the pets are more than six months old
  • Pets must meet the following conditions:
    • They are marked by implanting a transponder (chip) or by a clearly legible tattoo applied before July 3, 2011
    • They are vaccinated against rabies and the date of vaccination must not precede the date of application of the transponder or tattoo. You must wait 21 days from the date of the rabies vaccination before traveling
    • They are accompanied by a valid passport issued by an EU member state or a veterinary health certificate according to the model

Entering or returning to Bulgaria from an EU member state or from non-EU countries

    When entering or returning to Bulgaria, your pet must have:
  • a microchip
  • a rabies vaccination
  • a valid pet passport or veterinary certificate according to Reg.577-2013-EC.

For certain countries - Ukraine, Kazakhstan, Georgia, Armenia, Israel, Kuwait, etc., which are not listed in Reg.2020/2016-EC (see the full list), a rabies antibody titer test is required. The test must be performed in approved laboratories, which are listed in the following links (approved laboratories for rabies testing in Third Countries; Approved laboratories for rabies testing in the EU).
The results of the rabies antibody titer tests must be documented in the relevant section of the identification document. The rabies antibody titration test must be performed on a blood sample taken no earlier than 30 days after the date of vaccination and no less than three months before the date of travel.
